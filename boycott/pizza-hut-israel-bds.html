History of Pizza Hut

Founded in 1958 by brothers Dan and Frank Carney in Wichita, Kansas, Pizza Hut has grown from a single location into one of the world's largest pizza restaurant chains. Known for its iconic red roof and distinctive dine-in experience, Pizza Hut became a pioneer in the pizza delivery industry, introducing concepts such as the delivery guarantee and the iconic 'Pizza Hut' jingle. Over the years, the company expanded globally, reaching markets in over 100 countries, offering a diverse menu including pizza, pasta, wings, and desserts. Throughout its history, Pizza Hut has remained a cultural staple, continually innovating with new menu items and marketing campaigns, solidifying its position as a leader in the pizza industry.
